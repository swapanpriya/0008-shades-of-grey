Raise your complain.

  • Mail
  • Voice

General Tips:

  1. Be Clear and Concise: Keep your communication straightforward and to the point.
  2. Include Evidence: If possible, attach or mention any evidence that supports your report.
  3. Stay Calm and Polite: Maintain a professional tone in your communication.
  4. Provide Contact Information: Ensure the authorities can reach you for additional details or clarification.
  5. Follow Up: If you don't receive a response within a reasonable time frame, consider following up on your report.

Writing an Email to Report Cybercrimes:

  1. Subject Line: Use a clear and concise subject line that summarizes the issue.
  2. Introduction: Start with a polite greeting and a brief introduction mentioning the purpose of your email.
  3. Details: Provide specific details about the cybercrime incident, including: Any relevant evidence or supporting information (screenshots, emails, etc.) Date and time of the incident (if known) Description of what happened Any financial losses or damages incurred (if applicable)
  4. Contact Information: Include your contact information (name, email, phone number) for follow-up or additional questions.
  5. Conclusion: Express gratitude for their attention and request confirmation of receipt or any next steps.

Leaving a Voice Message to Report Cybercrimes:

  1. Introduction: Start by clearly stating your name and the purpose of your call.
  2. Details: Provide a concise but thorough description of the cybercrime incident, including the same details mentioned for the email.
  3. Contact Information: Speak slowly and clearly when leaving your contact information. Repeat it if necessary to ensure accuracy.
  4. Request for Follow-Up: Politely request a return call for further information or clarification if needed.
  5. Conclusion: Thank them for their attention and willingness to address the issue.
